The goal of this exercise is to <span class="text-[var(--color-text)]">understand how equipotential lines are drawn</span> in an isotropic medium and how they allow us to determine the <span class="text-[var(--color-text)]">direction of groundwater flow</span>.

## Statement
<span class="text-[var(--color-text)]">
A cylinder filled with a saturated porous medium allows water to flow under laminar conditions. Piezometers are inserted at different depths at both ends:
</span>

- <span class="text-[var(--titles)]">On the left side, all piezometers indicate a level of 20 cm</span>.  
- <span class="text-[var(--titles)]">On the right side, all indicate a level of 14 cm</span>.  

  <span class="text-[var(--titles)]">Based on this information:</span>

<span class="text-[var(--titles)]">1. Draw equipotential lines in the sand at 1 ft intervals.</span>  
<span class="text-[var(--titles)]">2. Determine the water level in a hypothetical piezometer placed in the center.</span>

## Interactive hint
- <span class="text-[var(--titles)]">Equipotential lines connect points with the same hydraulic head.</span>  
- <span class="text-[var(--titles)]">Flow is always perpendicular to equipotential lines.</span>  
- <span class="text-[var(--titles)]">Since the medium is homogeneous, the lines must be uniformly spaced.</span>

  <span class="text-[var(--titles)]">1. The equipotential lines must be horizontal and evenly spaced, since the medium is homogeneous and the flow is vertical.</span>
  <span class="text-[var(--titles)]">2. The water level in the central piezometer will rise to 14 ft</span>.
